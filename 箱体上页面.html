The 4 Stages of Anaerobic Digestion

1 Hydrolysis

Complex organic matter (carbohydrates, proteins, fats) is broken down into soluble molecules like sugars and amino acids by fermentative bacteria.

2 Acidogenesis

The soluble molecules are converted into volatile fatty acids (VFAs), ammonia, carbon dioxide, and hydrogen sulfide by acidogenic bacteria.

3 Acetogenesis

VFAs are further digested by acetogens to produce acetic acid, carbon dioxide, and hydrogen. This prepares the material for the final stage.

4 Methanogenesis

Methanogens (archaea) convert acetic acid and hydrogen into methane (CH₄) and carbon dioxide (CO₂)—the main components of biogas.
